// CYBER SAFETY TIPS

Stay Protected

Essential cybersecurity guidelines for Sri Lankan students, teachers, and parents — covering password security, safe browsing, social media safety, and device protection tailored to our national digital landscape.

// 01 — CYBER SAFETY TIPS

Essential Guidelines

Practical steps to protect yourself and your family in Sri Lanka's rapidly evolving digital environment.

// PASSWORD SECURITY

STRONG PASSWORDS

Use unique, complex passwords with at least 12 characters combining uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across accounts. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.

  • Use a password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password
  • Enable 2FA on all Sri Lankan banking apps (Commercial Bank, HNB, Sampath)
  • Never share passwords via WhatsApp or SMS
  • Change default router passwords immediately
// SAFE BROWSING

SECURE WEB PRACTICES

Always verify HTTPS on websites before entering credentials. Avoid clicking suspicious links in emails or messages. Use ad-blockers and keep browsers updated. Never download files from untrusted sources.

  • Verify .lk domains before entering personal data
  • Be cautious of fake government portals (gov.lk impersonations)
  • Use Brave Browser or Firefox with uBlock Origin
  • Check for the padlock icon before online transactions
// SOCIAL MEDIA

DIGITAL FOOTPRINT

Review privacy settings regularly on all platforms. Never share personal information publicly. Be cautious about accepting friend requests from strangers. Think before posting — digital content lasts forever.

  • Set profiles to private on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok
  • Never share Aadhaar/NIC numbers or home addresses
  • Be wary of fake scholarship pages targeting Sri Lankan students
  • Report suspicious accounts to platform moderators
// DEVICE PROTECTION

SECURE YOUR DEVICES

Install and update antivirus software. Enable automatic system updates. Lock devices with PINs, passwords, or biometrics. Back up important data regularly to secure cloud or offline storage.

  • Install Windows Defender or Kaspersky (free for students)
  • Enable Find My Device on Android and iOS
  • Encrypt sensitive files before cloud upload
  • Regularly back up to Google Drive or OneDrive
// NETWORK SAFETY

WIFI & PUBLIC NETWORKS

Avoid using public WiFi for sensitive transactions. Use a VPN when connecting to unknown networks. Secure your home WiFi with WPA3 encryption. Change default router passwords immediately.

  • Never use public WiFi at cafes for banking (Commercial Bank, HNB)
  • Use a reputable VPN like ProtonVPN or NordVPN
  • Disable auto-connect to open WiFi networks
  • Regularly check connected devices on your router
// AI & DEEPFAKE AWARENESS

EMERGING THREATS

AI-generated phishing emails and deepfake videos are now targeting Sri Lankan students and professionals. Learn to identify synthetic media through visual inconsistencies and source verification.

  • Verify unexpected requests via secondary channels (phone call)
  • Check for unnatural facial movements in video calls
  • Be skeptical of voice messages requesting urgent transfers
  • Report deepfake content to Sri Lanka CERT immediately
// 02 — QUICK CHECKLIST

Daily Security Habits

Simple routines to maintain digital hygiene every day.

MORNING ROUTINE

  • Check for software updates on all devices
  • Review email for suspicious messages
  • Verify banking app notifications
  • Scan USB drives before opening files
  • Check social media privacy settings

ONLINE SHOPPING

  • Use only trusted Sri Lankan e-commerce sites (Daraz, Kapruka)
  • Verify seller ratings and reviews
  • Use credit cards over debit cards for protection
  • Never save card details on websites
  • Check for HTTPS and secure payment gateways

SCHOOL & WORK

  • Log out of shared computers after use
  • Never share school login credentials
  • Use school VPN for remote access
  • Report suspicious emails to IT staff
  • Backup assignments to cloud storage

WHATSAPP & MESSAGING

  • Enable two-step verification on WhatsApp
  • Never click unknown links in group chats
  • Verify forwarded messages before sharing
  • Block and report spam numbers
  • Use disappearing messages for sensitive chats

BANKING & FINANCE

  • Never share OTPs with anyone
  • Use official banking apps only (not APKs)
  • Enable transaction alerts via SMS
  • Regularly review bank statements
  • Report unauthorized transactions immediately

EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS

  • Save Sri Lanka CERT hotline: +94 11 269 1694
  • Know your nearest police cybercrime unit
  • Keep screenshots of suspicious activity
  • Have backup email for account recovery
  • Know how to freeze bank accounts quickly
