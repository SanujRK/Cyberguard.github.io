// 02 — WHAT TO DO FIRST

Emergency Response

Immediate steps to take when you discover a cyber incident. Acting quickly can minimize damage.

01

STOP AND DISCONNECT

Immediately disconnect from the internet if you suspect malware or unauthorized access. Turn off WiFi or unplug the ethernet cable. Do not shut down the device if you need to preserve evidence.

02

DOCUMENT EVERYTHING

Take screenshots of suspicious emails, messages, or web pages. Note the date, time, and any URLs involved. Save all evidence before it disappears. This documentation is crucial for investigation.

03

CHANGE PASSWORDS

Change passwords for all affected accounts immediately from a clean device. Use unique, strong passwords. Enable 2FA if not already active. Check for unauthorized login activity in account settings.

04

CONTACT YOUR BANK

If financial information is compromised, contact your bank immediately (Commercial Bank, HNB, Sampath, etc.). Request to freeze accounts or block cards. File a fraud report with the bank's security team.

05

REPORT OFFICIALLY

File a report with Sri Lanka CERT (+94 11 269 1694) and the Police Cybercrime Division (+94 11 242 1111). For students, also notify your school principal and IT administrator. Use the form below for our portal.
